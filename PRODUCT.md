# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are students, staff, teachers, and system teachers in a school recycling activity. Students sign in with their student ID to view accumulated points and exchange history. Staff record waste exchanges and point adjustments during day-to-day operations. Teachers manage student accounts, waste point rules, rankings, reports, backups, and school-wide oversight.

Users often work on mobile devices at school, sometimes while handling queues of students and physical recyclable items. The interface must support quick scanning, clear confirmation, and reliable audit trails.

## Product Purpose

`สว รย รักษ์โลกและสิ่งแวดล้อม` helps the school run an online recycling-points program. It turns waste exchange records into student points, classroom rankings, dashboards, Excel reports, and backup files.

Success means teachers can create and maintain accounts for the whole school, staff can record exchanges accurately, and students can see their points without asking staff to check manually.

## Brand Personality

Clear, trustworthy, and encouraging. The system should feel school-friendly and environmental, but still behave like a dependable operational tool. Visual personality comes from the project logo, green environmental accents, and calm Thai copy rather than decorative effects.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing landing-page composition, oversized hero sections, decorative card-heavy layouts, playful effects that slow down data entry, and admin jargon such as "super admin" or "transaction log" when a Thai school term is clearer. Avoid exposing sensitive account internals such as password hashes in exports or backups.

## Design Principles

1. Make the next action obvious for each role.
2. Prefer Thai school language over technical admin terms.
3. Keep mobile workflows first-class, especially menus, forms, and tables.
4. Show review and confirmation before risky bulk changes.
5. Preserve auditability: exchanges, adjustments, reports, and backups should be traceable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible forms, visible focus states, readable Thai text, and layouts that do not require wide desktop screens. Avoid relying on color alone for status. Keep motion minimal and respect reduced-motion preferences when animation is introduced.
